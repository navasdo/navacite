Cochlea Compass

Cochlea Compass is designed to provide insight into how well students understand what they hear. It checks their ability to focus on spoken passages, remember key details, and grasp the main idea. Students answer questions that test memory, sequencing, and deeper thinking - like making inferences or predictions.

You may select up to 3 different listening difficulty levels and a maximum of 5 stories.

NOTE: This tool is for the clinician running the assessment. Please don’t share the screen with the student—just read the instructions and guide them through the activity.

Instructions:
1. Before each passage, say: "Listen carefully to the next paragraph. I can only play it once, and I’ll ask you questions afterward."
2. Always read the title aloud before starting.
3. After the passage plays, read the associated questions.
4. Passages should only be played once, but questions may be repeated if the student requests it or does not respond within 10 seconds.
5. Do not repeat a question if the student already answered incorrectly.
These directions are adapted from similar standardized tasks; use your professional judgment as needed.
